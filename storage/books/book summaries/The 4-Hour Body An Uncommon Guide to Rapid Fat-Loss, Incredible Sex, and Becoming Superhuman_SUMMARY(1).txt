### **BASEBALL: THE ARCHITECTURE OF BABE RUTH (Chapter 35)**

**Key Theme:** Elite hitting mechanics can be quantified and taught using angular measurements.

**Cevallos Swing Rating (CSR):**  
`CSR = 3(180 – E) + W`
- **E**: Elbow angle (smaller = better)
- **W**: Wrist angle (larger = better)

**Benefits of High CSR:**
- Tighter swing = better pitch selection (can’t reach outside strikes)
- Contact made further back = more time to assess pitch
- Longer Area of Impact (AOI) = higher consistency

**The Big Three Positions:**
1. **The Cushion**: Front heel down, optimal torque between shoulders/hips (Angle S ≈ 25°)
2. **The Slot**: Back elbow drops to side, spine stays vertical—kinetic link to legs/hips
3. **Impact**: E angle ≈ 80°, W angle ≈ 180°

### **SCIENTIFIC LITERACY: SPOTTING BAD SCIENCE (Chapters 42-44)**

**The Big Five Questions:**
1. **Is a relative change (%) used to convince?** Always ask for absolute numbers. "20% more fat loss" might mean 0.05 lbs.
2. **Is an observational study claiming causation?** Correlation ≠ causation (e.g., pirates vs. global warming).
3. **Does it depend on self-reporting?** People underestimate food intake by 20-30%; Women’s Health Initiative food questionnaires are unreliable.
4. **Is there a true control group?** Changing one macronutrient inevitably changes others; impossible to isolate variables.
5. **Do funders have vested interests?** Fred Stare’s sugar research funded by General Foods; James Hill’s obesity research funded by Coca-Cola.

**P-Value Explained:** <0.05 means <5% likelihood result is due to chance. But statistical significance ≠ practical significance.

**Pharma Tricks (Ben Goldacre):** Testing drugs in "winners" (younger, healthier subjects); using placebo controls; inadequate competitor dosing; hiding side effects; surrogate outcomes (cholesterol vs. cardiac deaths); data torturing; ignoring dropouts.

**Key Takeaway:** "Working long in the gym is often a form of laziness, an avoidance of hard thinking."
